Wilder Foundation

Wilder Foundation, since 1906, has had a mission to promote the social welfare of persons resident or located in the greater Saint Paul metropolitan area by all appropriate means. So, we have a local geographic focus where we need to create impact, an opportunity to do this work for the benefit of all and though an evolving set of means and actions.

When it comes to disseminating our Research work, our public web site has become the core means by which we accomplish this. It is simply the best tool we have for getting Wilder's body of research and analysis of evidence-based practices into the hands of those who might put this information to use within our region and beyond.

Wilder Research has been creating community impact through person-focused research since 1917. In fact, our very first study that year influenced policy change towards healthier living conditions for residents of St. Paul. In more recent times, maybe 20 years ago, we started creating our first digital catalog of reports and publications as desktop publishing software came into use. We used a desktop library software from InMagic called DB/TextWorks. When our first website was developed we then began to copy information and files there for those few early adopters of dial-up internet to discover. Over the years, and through several InMagic software upgrades and wilder.org redesign projects, we found ourselves largely still at a point where we were copying data and files into fairly general purpose web content systems, for what had become basically everyone with a computer or mobile device. We owed it to ourselves and our stakeholders to do something better.

Why InMagic Presto?

  • Internal tool for librarians
  • In use for almost 20 years
  • Effective and powerful
  • Lots of internal support and effort
So again, we first started using desktop software from InMagic to manage our catalog back before the year 2000. It proved to be an effective and powerful tool for managing and searching though our growing body of work, but it was mainly used by our Librarians. By around 2005, we installed a web publisher component that allowed for viewing the data via web browser. We ran this as an internal only service though, so while it allowed for a loose integration with our department's intranet and increased staff access, website visitors were still only getting manually duplicated data from content management systems that were not necessarily built to manage them well. In addition, we were managing multiple subsets of data in different places, in order to target different audiences or restrict permissions to certain content. This is how we operated for 3 iterations of wilder.org. Finally, a few years ago we decided it was time to upgrade to the SaaS version of InMagic Presto. In addition to consolidating all of the data, permissions, and access into one tool, this opened the door for the online data integration with Drupal on the wilder.org that we have today.
